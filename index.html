Background

My scientific research is based on understanding the relationship between the aberrant molecular pathways and the disease phenotypes in a myriad of cellular contexts.

During my PhD at University of Massachusetts Medical School, I performed one of the first global comparisons of higher-order nuclear organization between normal and breast cancer cells, and worked on the molecular effects of chromatin modifying factors in bone differentiation and cancer.

During my first postdoctoral position with Dr. John Rinn at Harvard University, my studies focused on the role of long non-coding RNAs on genome architecture.

To further my training and expand my research experience in the areas of RNA biology and neurodevelopmental diseases, I joined Dr. Benjamin Blencowe’s laboratory at University of Toronto.
